When I am speaking with someone who introduce himself as programmer or generally IT, I want to know what is he doing, some best practices, what technology does he / she like, and so on. But mostly it is really short conversation and we switch to some new topic. Why?

If someone likes for example cycling and meet other biker, they can talk with each other for a long time, for example which bike brand does the other guy have, did he tried the one I am ridding, what is his favorite place / biker, etc. Why doesn't this work with programmers? No one likes it that much?

Every day something happens. Every day there is something new and interesting.

Blogs

There are lot of blogs on internet. Big companies like Google or Facebook has their own developers blog, so you can read what's going on there. Even programmers have blogs. Let's say you are working as Android developer, then you can find someone who is working with Android too and has his own blog and from time to time visit it.

news.ycombinator.com

Or Hacker News, page with list of various topics from number of pages. You can find here posts from nearly every kind of IT world, like programming, system administration, graphics, etc.

Link : news.ycombinator.com

developers.googleblog.com

Link : developers.googleblog.com

developers.facebook.com/blog

blog.codinghorror.com

Blog from Jeff Atwood, co-founder of website Stack Overflow. Today I read couple of his posts, for example Why I'm The Best Programmer In The World, where you can read it is not bad to admit you are not genius. It is good to ask friends / colleagues how would they solve your problem, or interpret them your solution to see if they don't have any suggestions.

If someone corrects you, and you feel offended, then you have an ego problem.

Nouman Ali Khan

By asking and sharing your solutions and listening to "criticism" you move forward. And that's what it is all about. To be better than yesterday / year ago.

Link : blog.codinghorror.com

Java and Android

When I was working with Java and later with Android I found couple of good blogs with useful stuff. For Java language it is no doubt javarevisited.blogspot.sk from Javin Paul. Lot of posts about programming, design patterns, questions you can meet during job interviews, ... I can only recommend you to visit that page and go through it.

During Android app development, two pages really helped me which I remembered. The first is vogella.com from Lars Vogel and the second is mkyong.com from Yong Mook Kim.

Events

Google Code Jam

This is an international programming competition hosted by Google. It consist of a set of algorithmic problems and you can use any programming language. If you are curious about the problems, you can visit their page and look at past problems.

Link : code.google.com/codejam

WebExpo

I attended this event last year ( 2015 ) and it was really good. Lecturers from Microsoft, Oracle, Twitter, Parallax, ... speaking about their work, how they are solving problems, or what features comes with new version of their product and how to use it.

Link : webexpo.net

Brno PHP

This action is not that big and good as WebExpo but still interesting.

Link : brnophp.cz
